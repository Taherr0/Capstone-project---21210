Project Introduction

The Challenge

Egypt produces nearly 90 million tons of solid waste annually, yet only a small percentage is recycled. Electronic waste, in particular, contains hazardous materials that contribute to environmental pollution and public health risks when improperly disposed of. At the same time, these discarded electronics contain valuable components that are rarely reused.

Previous Solutions

Existing solutions mainly focus on large-scale recycling systems that require advanced infrastructure, specialized facilities, and high financial investment. While effective in developed countries, these approaches are difficult to implement in developing nations such as Egypt due to cost and accessibility limitations.

Proposed Solution

Our project proposes a sustainable and low-cost alternative by reusing functional components from electronic waste to construct a stable 12V DC power supply. This approach reduces waste, lowers costs, and provides a practical energy solution suitable for school laboratories and small-scale applications.

Project Analysis

Voltage Stability

The voltage stability of the power supply was tested under loads ranging from 0% to 100%. Results show excellent performance with an average output of 12.04 V and minimal standard deviation. All load conditions passed.
