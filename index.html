Project Abstract

From 90 million tons of solid waste annually, Egypt only recycles 12% of household solid waste. This results in a huge amount of wasted materials and waste pollution. Thus, our study aims to address the issues of electronic waste and the effective management of waste resources. Using old waste materials, we seek to construct an effective 12V DC power supply to meet lab requirements from E-waste materials working in at least 2 school labs with a maximum error of ±5%. With a circuit board and key materials of a voltage regulator and a bridge rectifier, which was constructed from old materials like fans and chargers. As a result, our project succeeded in stabilizing production of output voltage across various loads with an average efficiency of 72.5% and a maximum voltage of 40V. Also, our project shows low energy loss and thermal efficiency with a maximum temperature difference of 27.5°C and a ripple of 0.36% at 100% load. Hence, our project shows a sustainable way to produce energy from e-waste in a functional device unit. For future research, we recommend use a high efficiency switching regulator That is estimated to raise efficiency to 74.3%.
