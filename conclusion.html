Conclusion & Recommendations

Conclusion

Our power supply outputs stable and efficient DC current using scientific design methods. Testing under different load conditions shows an average voltage of 12.04 V with a minimal standard deviation of 0.008 V and an average error of 0.78%, indicating highly consistent performance. Ripple voltage remained well below design limits: +12 V rail measured 43 mV, +5 V rail 18 mV, and +3.3 V rail 21 mV. Temperature rise was evaluated, showing a maximum ΔT of 27.5°C at full load, which is below 40°C, confirming safe operation. Efficiency ranged from 70.48% at 25% load to 74.30% at 50% load, with an average of 72.5%. Overall, the prototype meets all design requirements for voltage stability, output quality, thermal safety, and energy efficiency.

Recommendations

  • Convert excess heat from the power supply into usable energy using reductive decay methods.
  • Integrate automatic overheating detection that introduces helium gas for cooling sensitive components.
  • Use new components instead of reused ones to avoid hidden faults, reduced efficiency, and safety risks.
  • Construct a stronger protective structure using durable metals to ensure the device is safe from damage.
